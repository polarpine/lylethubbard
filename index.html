Lyle Turner Hubbard Jr. (Bobby)

I have been a follower of Lyle from birth. My big brother got his nickname stolen by me, and never forgave me until I started giving him free golf lessons. Admiring my big brother started with watching him throw a golf club so high into a fir tree that it is still probably there! Then there was his winning a trophy (still in the case at Franklin H.S.) for “Alchemist of the Year” by blowing up the Chemistry Lab. I was told by a teacher there that one piece of glass split a bench in half. So of course I became a chemist.

When he went off to college I kind of lost track of him. That was partly because he took a bus ride to California but managed to get lost and never found it. Then he was off to Colorado, Washington, Alaska, Tennessee, Alaska again, and finally back to the Northwest. I don’t think he ever found California. His golf balls still can’t find fairways either, despite two Masters and a Ph.D.

If you have never received a letter from Lyle you have missed the most clever and hilarious twisted treatment of the English language. Never get into a pun contest with him. I was just not interested in biology until he buried me in an avalanche of books and articles written by the best in the field; some that he knows personally. There are too few people that are as interesting to talk to until early in the morning. Lyle has mellowed over the years but has lost none of his passion about everything. He is still playing softball to win, and digging in the dirt to learn more about the history of animals and humans on this planet.

- Bob Hubbard
